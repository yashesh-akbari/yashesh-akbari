Terms and Conditions for Blood Donation - India:

  1. Eligibility:
  • Donors must be between the ages of 18 and 65 years.
  • Donors must weigh at least 50 kilograms.
  • Donors must be in good health and free from any infectious diseases such as HIV, hepatitis, and malaria.
  • Donors must not have donated blood within the last 3 months (for whole blood donation).
  1. Identification:
  • Donors must provide valid government-issued identification, such as Aadhaar Card, Voter ID, or Passport, for verification purposes.
  1. Health Assessment:
  • Donors will undergo a brief health screening process to ensure eligibility for blood donation.
  • Donors will be asked about their medical history, recent travel, and lifestyle factors that may affect blood safety.
  1. Voluntary Donation:
  • Blood donation is voluntary, and donors are not compensated financially for their donation.
  1. Confidentiality:
  • Donor information will be kept confidential and used only for the purpose of blood donation and related healthcare activities.
  1. Informed Consent:
  • Donors must provide informed consent before donating blood, indicating their understanding of the donation process and potential risks involved.
  1. Precautions:
  • Donors should eat a nutritious meal and drink plenty of fluids before donating blood to prevent fainting or dizziness.
  • Donors are advised to avoid strenuous physical activity immediately after donation.
  1. Donation Process:
  • Donors will be guided through the donation process by trained healthcare professionals.
  • The blood collection process typically takes around 10-15 minutes for whole blood donation.
  1. Post-Donation Care:
  • Donors will be provided with refreshments and advised to rest for a short period after donation.
  • Donors will receive instructions for post-donation care and contact information for any concerns or complications.
  1. Blood Testing:
    • All donated blood will undergo screening tests for infectious diseases to ensure the safety of the blood supply.
  2. Donation Frequency:
    • Donors can donate whole blood every 3 months, subject to eligibility criteria.
  3. Rights of Refusal:
    • Blood banks reserve the right to refuse blood donation from individuals who do not meet eligibility criteria or pose a risk to blood safety.

By agreeing to these Terms and Conditions, donors acknowledge their understanding and acceptance of the guidelines outlined above and consent to participate in the blood donation process in accordance with Indian regulations and standards.
